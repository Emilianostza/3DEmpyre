## Category 1: Direct Competitors (Managed 3D Capture Services)

### Matterport

- **What**: 3D space capture (rooms, buildings) — NOT object-level
- **Market**: Real estate, architecture, hospitality
- **Pricing**: $69-$309/month + per-space fees
- **Strengths**: Brand recognition, massive install base, easy-to-use cameras
- **Weakness for us**: They capture SPACES, not individual objects/products
- **Lesson**: Their onboarding flow and pricing page are best-in-class

### Scanblue

- **What**: Industrial 3D scanning and digitization services (Germany-based)
- **Market**: Manufacturing, automotive, quality inspection
- **Strengths**: High accuracy, industrial credibility
- **Weakness**: Not targeting restaurants/food, premium pricing
- **Lesson**: Their enterprise positioning and quality messaging

### AR Code (ar-code.com)

- **What**: AR QR codes with 3D object capture for restaurants
- **Market**: Restaurants, retail
- **Strengths**: Direct competitor to our restaurant offering, has AR QR code integration
- **Weakness**: Limited to basic 3D capture, no managed service (DIY)
- **Lesson**: They have good restaurant-specific marketing, study their messaging

### MenuAR (menuar.world)

- **What**: AR food menu platform
- **Market**: Restaurants, cafes, museums
- **Strengths**: Focused entirely on food AR menus
- **Weakness**: Appears to be smaller scale, limited geographic reach
- **Lesson**: Pure-play food AR — validates our market but they lack managed capture

### Reality Menu

- **What**: AR menu platform for restaurants
- **Market**: US restaurants
- **Strengths**: Simple QR-to-AR flow
- **Weakness**: Self-service model, quality depends on restaurant's photos
- **Lesson**: The Rail (restaurant industry media) featured them — good PR strategy

## Category 2: DIY / Self-Service Platforms (Indirect Competitors)

### Polycam

- **What**: 3D scanning app (LiDAR + photogrammetry)
- **Market**: Prosumers, small businesses, creators
- **Pricing**: Free tier + Pro subscription
- **Strengths**: Cross-platform, easy to use, both LiDAR and photo modes
- **Weakness**: Quality depends on user skill, no managed service
- **Why we're different**: We deliver GUARANTEED quality — they're DIY

### Scaniverse (Niantic)

- **What**: Free 3D scanning app, Apple-only
- **Market**: Consumers, hobbyists
- **Pricing**: Free
- **Strengths**: Fast on-device processing, owned by Niantic (Pokemon GO)
- **Weakness**: Apple-only, consumer-grade quality, no business features
- **Why we're different**: Enterprise quality vs consumer quality

### Luma AI

- **What**: Was 3D scanning (NeRF/3DGS), now pivoting to video AI (Dream Machine)
- **Market**: Creators, developers
- **Pricing**: Credits-based for Dream Machine
- **Strengths**: Early pioneer in neural 3D scanning
- **Weakness**: ABANDONING 3D scanning focus — mobile app no longer supports Gaussian Splatting
- **Lesson**: Market signal — pure consumer 3D scanning is not profitable enough alone

### RealityScan (Epic Games)

- **What**: Free photogrammetry app → exports to Sketchfab
- **Market**: Game developers, 3D artists
- **Strengths**: Epic Games backing, free, good quality
- **Weakness**: Targeted at game assets, not business use

### Meshy

- **What**: AI text/image to 3D model
- **Market**: Game developers, creators
- **Pricing**: Credits-based ($20-$100/month)
- **Strengths**: Fast generation, no scanning needed
- **Weakness**: NOT photorealistic — unsuitable for food/product marketing
- **Why we're different**: Real captures vs AI-generated (quality gap is huge)

## Category 3: Enterprise/Professional Photogrammetry Services

### Pix4D

- **What**: Professional photogrammetry software + PIX4Dengine API
- **Market**: Surveying, construction, agriculture (aerial/drone)
- **Strengths**: Industry standard, cloud API available
- **Weakness**: Focused on aerial/mapping, not object-level product scanning

### Autodesk (Reality Capture API)

- **What**: Cloud photogrammetry processing
- **Market**: AEC (Architecture, Engineering, Construction)
- **Strengths**: Enterprise-grade, integrates with Autodesk ecosystem
- **Weakness**: Enterprise pricing, complex integration

### DroneDeploy

- **What**: Drone-based 3D mapping
- **Market**: Construction, agriculture, inspections
- **Not relevant**: Aerial focus, not object-level

## Market Gaps We Can Own

1. **No one offers fully automated, managed end-to-end 3D capture for restaurants at scale**
   - DIY tools exist but quality varies wildly
   - Enterprise tools exist but target construction/surveying
   - We fill the gap: white-glove quality + scalable automation

2. **No one combines multiple capture technologies intelligently**
   - Photogrammetry for food/products
   - 3DGS for instant previews
   - LiDAR for spaces (future)
   - One platform, best tool for each job

3. **No one focuses on the EU restaurant market specifically**
   - Most competitors are US-based
   - EU restaurants are adopting digital menus rapidly
   - We're already on the ground in 3 EU countries

4. **Quality guarantee with SLA**
   - DIY tools = unpredictable quality
   - We guarantee sub-mm accuracy, optimized for web/AR, fast load times
   - Quality is THE differentiator

5. **Vertical expansion from a restaurant base**
   - Start with restaurants (proven demand) → expand to retail, real estate, museums
   - One capture team, multiple verticals

## What to Learn from the Best

### Landing Pages

- **Matterport**: Clean, interactive 3D demo above the fold
- **Stripe**: Dark theme, gradient accents, clear value prop in 5 words
- **Linear**: Minimal, fast, premium feel
- **Polycam**: Gallery of real captures builds trust immediately

### Customer Experience

- **Matterport**: Simple pricing, self-serve + enterprise tiers
- **Polycam**: Free tier → paid conversion funnel
- **Domino's**: AR as marketing tool, not just product feature

### Quality Signaling

- Sub-millimeter accuracy claims
- Before/after comparisons
- Real-world deployment numbers
- Named client testimonials with metrics
- "See it yourself" interactive demos
